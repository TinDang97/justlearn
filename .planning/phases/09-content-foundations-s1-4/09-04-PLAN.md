**lesson-09-outlier-detection.md** — "Detecting Outliers"
Duration: 40 minutes. Level: Intermediate.
Content:
- Part 1: What is an outlier? — statistical definition (IQR method, Z-score method) vs domain knowledge definition (negative revenue is always wrong)
- Part 2: IQR method — compute Q1, Q3, IQR = Q3-Q1; outliers are < Q1 - 1.5*IQR or > Q3 + 1.5*IQR
- Part 3: Z-score method — `(value - mean) / std`; values with |z| > 3 are suspect
- Part 4: Domain validation — some outliers are logical impossibilities (negative quantity, revenue > max product price * units); use boolean conditions
- Part 5: What to do with outliers — flag (add 'is_outlier' column), cap (clip to bounds), remove (drop), investigate (manual review); never silently delete without logging
- Use `<Warning>`: "Never automatically remove outliers in a production pipeline. Flag them first, investigate, then decide. Removing real data is unrecoverable."
